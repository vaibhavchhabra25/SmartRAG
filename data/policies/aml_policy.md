# Anti-Money Laundering (AML) Policy

## §1 Purpose and Scope
This policy establishes the bank's framework for detecting, preventing, and reporting money laundering and terrorist financing activity. It applies to all retail, commercial, and correspondent banking business lines.

## §2 Risk-Based Approach
The bank applies a risk-based approach to AML compliance. Each customer is assigned a risk rating (Low, Medium, High) at onboarding based on: country of residence/business, industry sector, product usage, expected transaction volume, and source of funds. High-risk customers (e.g., those from FATF grey-list jurisdictions, politically exposed persons (PEPs), or cash-intensive businesses) require Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD) and senior management sign-off before account opening.

## §3 Customer Risk Rating Review Frequency
- Low risk: reviewed every 36 months.
- Medium risk: reviewed every 24 months.
- High risk: reviewed every 12 months, or immediately upon a trigger event (e.g., adverse media hit, sanctions list match, unexplained transaction pattern change).

## §4 Suspicious Activity Reporting (SAR)
Any employee who identifies activity inconsistent with a customer's known profile, or that has no apparent lawful purpose, must escalate to the AML Investigations Unit within 24 hours via the internal case management system. The AML Investigations Unit has 30 calendar days to complete its review and, if warranted, file a Suspicious Activity Report with the relevant financial intelligence unit. Employees must never disclose to a customer that a SAR has been filed or is being considered ("tipping off" is a criminal offense).

## §5 Cash Transaction Thresholds
Any single cash transaction of $10,000 or more, or multiple related cash transactions that aggregate to $10,000 or more within a single business day, must be reported via a Currency Transaction Report (CTR), regardless of whether the activity appears suspicious.

## §6 Structuring
Structuring — deliberately breaking up transactions to stay under reporting thresholds — is illegal and must be reported as suspicious activity regardless of the dollar amount involved.

## §7 Training
All client-facing staff must complete AML training within 30 days of hire and annually thereafter. Completion is tracked in the Learning Management System and audited quarterly.
