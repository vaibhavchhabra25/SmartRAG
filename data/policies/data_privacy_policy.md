# Customer Data Privacy & Confidentiality Policy

## §1 Purpose
This policy governs how employees collect, access, use, and protect customer personal and financial information (PII), in line with applicable privacy regulations (e.g., GLBA, state privacy laws, and relevant international frameworks for cross-border operations).

## §2 Minimum Necessary Access
Employees may only access customer PII to the extent necessary to perform their job function. Browsing customer records out of curiosity, or accessing accounts of family members, friends, or public figures without a legitimate business reason, is strictly prohibited and subject to disciplinary action up to termination, regardless of whether any data was shared externally.

## §3 Internal Knowledge Assistant Use
Internal knowledge tools, including this assistant, are intended for answering questions about bank policies, procedures, and regulatory requirements. They must never be used to look up, store, repeat, or process specific customer account numbers, balances, transaction histories, Social Security numbers, or other individual customer PII. Any request of that nature must be declined and redirected to the appropriate case management or core banking system, which has proper access controls and audit logging.

## §4 Sharing Customer Information
Customer information may not be shared with third parties outside the bank without the customer's consent, except where required by law (e.g., regulatory reporting, valid subpoena) or under an existing service provider agreement with appropriate confidentiality protections.

## §5 Data Breach Reporting
Any suspected unauthorized access, loss, or disclosure of customer data must be reported to the Information Security team immediately, and in no case later than 24 hours after discovery. Delayed reporting may itself constitute a policy violation independent of the underlying incident.

## §6 Vendor and Third-Party Risk
Third parties with access to customer data must undergo a privacy and security risk assessment before onboarding and are subject to periodic re-assessment (at least every 2 years) and contractual data protection obligations.

## §7 Retention and Disposal
Customer PII must be retained only as long as necessary for business or legal purposes and disposed of securely (e.g., cryptographic erasure, certified destruction) once retention requirements expire.
