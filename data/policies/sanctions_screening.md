# Sanctions Screening Policy

## §1 Purpose
This policy governs how the bank screens customers, counterparties, and transactions against applicable sanctions lists to prevent dealings with sanctioned individuals, entities, and jurisdictions.

## §2 Lists Screened
At minimum, the bank screens against: the OFAC Specially Designated Nationals (SDN) List, the OFAC Consolidated Sanctions List, the UN Security Council Sanctions List, and the EU Consolidated Sanctions List. Screening also covers comprehensively sanctioned jurisdictions (currently designated by Compliance and updated as government guidance changes).

## §3 Screening Triggers
Sanctions screening occurs: (a) at customer onboarding, before the account is opened; (b) in real time for every outbound and inbound wire transfer; (c) on a recurring batch basis (nightly) against the full customer base, to catch list updates; and (d) whenever beneficial ownership or counterparty information changes.

## §4 Match Handling
A screening hit is classified as either a "true match" (confirmed identity match to a sanctioned party) or a "false positive" (name similarity only, no underlying identity match). All hits — true or potential — must be reviewed by a trained sanctions analyst before any transaction proceeds. Transactions with unresolved potential matches are held, not processed, pending review. True matches must be blocked and escalated to the Sanctions Compliance Officer immediately; under U.S. law, true OFAC matches generally must be blocked and reported to OFAC within 10 business days.

## §5 No Tipping Off
Customers must not be informed that they (or a counterparty) have been flagged, screened against, or blocked due to a sanctions match, except to the extent required to process a legitimate transaction rejection without revealing the underlying sanctions concern.

## §6 Escalation Authority
Only the Sanctions Compliance Officer or their delegate may authorize release of a held transaction after a false-positive determination is documented. Frontline staff and relationship managers have no authority to override a sanctions hold.

## §7 Recordkeeping
All sanctions screening decisions, including false-positive clearances, must be logged with analyst name, timestamp, and rationale, and retained for 5 years.
