# Regulatory & Compliance FAQ

## §1 What is the difference between AML and sanctions compliance?
AML (Anti-Money Laundering) compliance focuses on detecting and reporting potentially illicit financial activity such as money laundering and terrorist financing, regardless of who the parties are. Sanctions compliance focuses specifically on preventing the bank from doing business with designated individuals, entities, or jurisdictions on government sanctions lists. The two programs overlap operationally (e.g., a sanctioned party's activity is also AML-relevant) but have distinct legal bases, lists, and escalation paths — see the AML Policy and Sanctions Screening Policy.

## §2 Who can approve onboarding a high-risk or PEP customer?
Per the KYC Procedures, onboarding a Politically Exposed Person or other high-risk customer requires Enhanced Due Diligence and sign-off from a member of the Compliance leadership team. Frontline staff and branch managers do not have authority to approve these accounts independently.

## §3 What should I do if a customer asks whether they are under investigation?
Never confirm or deny that a SAR has been filed, that an account is under sanctions review, or that an investigation is ongoing. "Tipping off" a customer is prohibited under the AML Policy and Sanctions Screening Policy and can itself be a criminal offense. Politely decline to discuss the matter and escalate the customer's inquiry to Compliance.

## §4 Can I look up a customer's account balance using this assistant?
No. Per the Data Privacy Policy, this assistant is for policy and procedure questions only, not for retrieving individual customer account data. Use the core banking or case management system for that, where access is properly logged and controlled.

## §5 Is this assistant allowed to give investment or legal advice?
No. This assistant answers questions about internal bank policy and regulatory procedure. It does not provide investment recommendations, legal advice, or case-specific compliance determinations (e.g., "should we file a SAR on this specific customer?"). Those determinations require a qualified Compliance officer or legal counsel reviewing the specific facts.

## §6 How often are compliance policies reviewed and updated?
Compliance policies are reviewed at least annually, or sooner in response to regulatory change, audit findings, or material incidents. The version and effective date of a policy should always be confirmed in the official policy repository, since this knowledge base may lag the latest approved revision.

## §7 What is Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD)?
EDD is a deeper level of customer review applied to higher-risk relationships (e.g., PEPs, high-risk jurisdictions, cash-intensive businesses). It typically includes verifying source of wealth and source of funds, obtaining senior management approval, and applying more frequent ongoing monitoring, as described in the AML Policy and KYC Procedures.
