# Transaction Monitoring Policy

## §1 Purpose
This policy describes how the bank monitors customer transactions on an ongoing basis to detect potentially suspicious activity that may indicate money laundering, fraud, or other financial crime.

## §2 Monitoring System
All transactions flow through the automated transaction monitoring system, which applies a library of scenario-based rules (e.g., rapid movement of funds, round-dollar transactions, high-risk-jurisdiction wires, structuring patterns, dormant-account reactivation) and generates alerts for review when thresholds are breached.

## §3 Alert Triage
Generated alerts are triaged by Tier 1 analysts within 5 business days of generation. Each alert is classified as "no further action," "escalate to investigation," or "request additional information from relationship manager." Tier 1 decisions are subject to quality-control sampling by a Tier 2 reviewer.

## §4 Escalation to Investigations
Alerts escalated to the AML Investigations Unit must be assigned an investigator within 2 business days and resolved within 30 calendar days of escalation, consistent with the bank's SAR filing timelines under the AML Policy. Investigators document their analysis, supporting evidence, and conclusion in the case management system.

## §5 Scenario Tuning and Validation
Monitoring scenarios and thresholds are reviewed at least annually by the Model Risk and Compliance teams, using a combination of statistical tuning (above-the-line/below-the-line testing) and qualitative review, to balance detection effectiveness against alert volume and false-positive rates. Material changes to scenarios require sign-off from the Head of Financial Crime Compliance.

## §6 High-Risk Customer Monitoring
Customers rated High Risk under the AML Policy's risk-based approach are subject to lower alert thresholds and more frequent automated review cycles than Medium- or Low-risk customers.

## §7 Independent Testing
An independent function (Internal Audit or a designated second-line team) tests the transaction monitoring program's effectiveness at least annually, including sample testing of closed alerts and assessment of scenario coverage against the bank's current risk profile.
