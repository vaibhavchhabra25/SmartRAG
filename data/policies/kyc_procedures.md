# Know Your Customer (KYC) Procedures

## §1 Individual Customer Identification
At account opening, all individual customers must provide: full legal name, date of birth, residential address, and a government-issued photo ID (passport, driver's license, or national ID card). Identity must be verified using a reliable, independent source document, data, or information — either documentary verification (physical ID review) or non-documentary verification (e.g., credit bureau check) where permitted.

## §2 Corporate Customer Identification
For business accounts, the bank must collect: legal entity name, business address, taxpayer identification number, and formation documents (articles of incorporation or equivalent). The bank must also identify and verify the identity of each beneficial owner who owns 25% or more of the entity, and one individual with significant managerial control (the "control prong"), per the Beneficial Ownership Rule.

## §3 Politically Exposed Persons (PEPs)
Customers identified as PEPs — current or former senior government officials, their immediate family, and close associates — automatically require Enhanced Due Diligence regardless of stated risk factors, including verification of source of wealth and source of funds, and approval from a member of the Compliance leadership team.

## §4 Ongoing Monitoring
KYC information is not a one-time exercise. Relationship managers must refresh customer due diligence files: every 1 year for high-risk customers, every 2 years for medium-risk, and every 3 years for low-risk, or sooner if a material change in the customer's profile is identified (e.g., large unexplained deposits, change in business activity, adverse media).

## §5 Simplified Due Diligence
Simplified Due Diligence may apply to low-risk, low-value retail accounts (e.g., basic savings accounts under $5,000 with no international wire activity), subject to documented risk assessment and Compliance approval of the simplified program.

## §6 Account Opening Decline / Exit
Compliance may instruct a line of business to decline onboarding or exit an existing relationship where KYC cannot be completed, where the customer is unwilling to provide required information, or where ongoing monitoring reveals risk inconsistent with the bank's risk appetite. Such decisions must be documented in the case management system with rationale.

## §7 Record Retention
All KYC documentation, including identity verification records and beneficial ownership certifications, must be retained for a minimum of 5 years after account closure, per regulatory record-keeping requirements.
